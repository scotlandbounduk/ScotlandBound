How to Use This Guide

The Travel Profile

We originally mapped this out for two adults with no mobility issues. However, consider this a rock-solid baseline. It serves as an excellent starting point whether you are traveling solo, in a large group, or need to adapt the pacing for specific accessibility requirements.

Intentionally Free Slots

You’ll notice our city days are left broad and uncrowded. One of the greatest joys of Scottish towns is simply walking, people-watching, and getting a little lost. We didn't want to over-schedule your time with our exact tastes at the expense of your own random, unforgettable discoveries.

Food & Stays

Apart from a few "you have to try this" local spots, we have left dining choices up to you, as everyone's budget, accommodation location, and dietary habits differ. For lodging, we highly recommend looking into local B&Bs and self-catering options as they are significantly more cost-effective and offer a far more private, authentic experience.

Highland Single-Track Driving Protocol

Much of the deep Western Highlands and Northern NC500 stretches utilize single-track roads. Always check ahead for passing places, let faster vehicles behind you pass by pulling into laybys on your left, and maintain high spatial awareness over blind crests.

Midge Awareness Advice

Highland midges are common in valleys like Glen Etive and Glencoe if the wind drops completely still. Keep doors shut when leaving the car and apply protective repellant before stepping out.
